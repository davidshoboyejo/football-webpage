Serie A Star Players

Introduction

The Serie A has been home to some of the best football players of all time in a range of different positions. In this section we will look at just a few of these players.

Gianluigi Buffon

It's hard to think of the Serie A without thinking of this man, Gianluigi Buffon. Buffon holds multiple records in the Serie A including, most titles won by an individual player, oldest player to win a title and also most appearances of all-time. These records alone show how much of an influential goalkeeper Buffon was and also the mark he left on the Serie A. Buffon not only had success in the Serie A league but also had international success with the Italian National team with him being part of a Fifa World Cup winning team in 2006.

Giorgio Chiellini

Giorgio Chiellini is widely regarded as one of the greatest defenders in Serie A history, known for his leadership, defensive prowess, and consistency over a career that spanned nearly two decades. Similarly to Buffon, Chiellini spent his entire Serie A career at Juventus leading them to 9 Serie A titles. At Euro 2020, Chiellini also left his mark on Italian football by playing a key role in the Italian National Team's victory over England in the final.

Francesco Totti

When it comes to goal scoring records in the Serie A, there is no better man in recent times than Francesco Totti. Like Buffon and Chiellini, Totti gave his loyalty to just one Serie A club over his entire career but in this case his loyalties lied with AS Roma. Totti has also had a record breaking career with him holding the records for Roma's all-time top scorer and Serie A's all-time top scorer for a single club. Although Totti never won consistent Serie A titles, he did play a key role in Roma's 2000-2001 Scudetto win, one of the club's most memorable achievements, when he helped lead Roma to the league championship.

Andrea Pirlo

Andrea Pirlo is widely regarded as one of the finest midfielders in Serie A history, known for his incredible vision, passing ability, and mastery of the game from a deep-lying playmaker role. His success in Serie A, which spanned over two decades, was defined by numerous individual achievements and collective trophies, particularly with Juventus and AC Milan. Pirlo has been consistently recognised as one of Serie A's greatest ever midfielders with him being named Serie A Footballer of the Year in 2012 and also being included in the FIFA World Cup All-Star Squad in 2006.
