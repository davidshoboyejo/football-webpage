Ligue 1 History

Foundation of Division 1

France's top division was established in 1932 under the name Division 1. The inaugural season featured 20 teams competing for the championship title. The league underwent a significant rebranding in 2002, when it was renamed to Ligue 1.

Founding Members

The original 20 teams that competed in the first season were: Antibes, CA Paris, Cannes, Club Français, Excelsior AC Roubaix, Fives, Hyères, Marseille, Metz, Mulhouse, Nice, Nîmes, Alès, Lille, Racing Club de France, Red Star Olympique, Rennes, Sochaux, Sète and Montpellier.

Modern Era

Today, Ligue 1 features 18 teams competing for the championship. The reduction from 20 to 18 teams represents one of the many modernization efforts the league has undergone since its inception. The competition maintains high standards of play and continues to produce world-class talent.

Most Successful Clubs

Paris Saint-Germain (PSG) leads the all-time rankings with 12 league titles, with an impressive run of 10 championships in the last 12 years. Saint-Etienne follows with 10 titles, while Marseille holds third place with 9 championship victories.
