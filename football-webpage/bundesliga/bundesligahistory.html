Bundesliga History

Foundation of the Bundesliga

The Bundesliga was founded in July 1962 as the top tier of German football. The creation of the league was prompted by the national team's disappointing performance in the World Cup, leading to a restructuring of German football.

Founding Members

16 teams agreed to become founding members of the Bundesliga: Munich, Cologne, Borussia Dortmund, Eintracht Braunschweig, Eintracht Frankfurt, Hamburg, Hertha Berlin, Kaiserslautern, Karlsruhe, Preußen Munster, MSV Duisburg, Nuremberg, Saarbrücken, Schalke, VfB Stuttgart and Werder Bremen. Cologne made history by becoming the first ever Bundesliga champions.

Modern Era

Today, the Bundesliga features 18 teams competing for the championship. The competition uses a relegation system where the bottom three teams are replaced by the top three from Germany's second tier each season. Over its history, 58 different teams have competed in the Bundesliga.

Most Successful Clubs

Bayern Munich dominates the Bundesliga's history with an incredible 32 championship titles. Behind them, Borussia Dortmund and Borussia Mönchengladbach are tied for second place with 5 titles each.
