Serie A History

Early History (1898-1929)

From 1898 to 1922, Italian football was organized into regional groups. Due to growing participation, the Italian Football Federation (FIGC) split the CCI in 1921, leading to the formation of Lega Nord in Milan - the predecessor of today's Lega Serie A. After CCI teams rejoined, FIGC created two interregional divisions. In 1926, internal changes and fascist pressures led to southern teams joining the national division.

Modern Serie A (1929-Present)

Serie A as we know it today began in the 1929-1930 season. The championship title is known as the "scudetto" ("small shield"), as since 1923-24 the winners wear the Italian tricolour shield on their jerseys the following season. Since 2004-05, the Coppa Campioni d'Italia trophy is awarded on the pitch after the final match.

Most Successful Clubs

Juventus leads with 36 championships, followed by Inter Milan (20) and AC Milan (19), establishing themselves as the most dominant forces in Italian football history.

Recent Developments

In February 2024, Serie A clubs voted on potentially reducing the league from 20 to 18 teams. Sixteen clubs voted against the reduction, maintaining the current 20-team format.
